Adepta Sororitas Squad

The shuttle's systems failed during the final approach. With no sign of rescue, the survivors must make their way through the Underhive if they're going to return to civilisation.

Squad Composition

Minimum 3 fighters.

The squad may have 0-1 Superior.

Sister Famulous

Sister Hospitaller

Faithful

Recruitment

Cut off as they are, the Sister Superior, Sister Famulous, Sister Hospitaller, Retributor and Battle Sisters may only be recruited before the squad's first battle. The Faithful may only be recruited after the squad wins a battle.

Hired Guns may only be recruited if the squad includes a Sister Famulous, and no Wyrd or other mutant may be hired for any reason.

The Emperor Protects

Once per battle, a single Witch Hunter may gain the benefit of any one of the following Minor Wyrd Powers: Nullify Power, Zen Shootist, Lucky Aura, Healing Hands.

Territory

An Adepta Sororitas Squad relies on the aid of loyal worshippers of the Emperor as they make their way up the Underhive. After every battle, roll once on the Redemptionist Territories table. The squad does not need to assign a fighter to work the territory to gain the benefits.

As strangers to the Underhive the Adepta Sororitas cannot earn income by foraging, but receive enough food from loyalists to avoid starvation.

Leadership

If a Sister Superior dies, she passes her Leadership ability to the most experienced Battle Sister. If all Witch Hunters die, the surviving Faithful may become a Redemptionist Mob.

Capture

If an Adepta Sororitas Squad captures a Wyrd, Spook user, Scaly or fighter with a mutation, you may not exchange the captive - the other gang must attempt a rescue mission or allow the mutant to be put to death.

Any Redemptionist gang that captures a Witch Hunter will release her unharmed - treat the result as a Full Recovery. If a Witch Hunter is captured and you cannot secure her release through exchange, you must attempt a rescue.
